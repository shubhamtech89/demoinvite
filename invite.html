About Special Forces Adventures

Special Forces Adventures (SFA) is the outcome of a vision to bring the skills of elite ex-Special Forces soldiers into the realm of Adventure sports and Media production. A unique blend of Adventure, Survival Skills and training to further the ‘Adventure’ in adventure sports and to provide consultancy services to Media houses for military / Special Forces related productions.

We are a group of highly trained Special Forces combat veterans from the Army Special Forces (Para Commandos) and the MARCO’s (Navy Marine Commandos). Each individual instructor holds decades of experience in Adventure, Survival and Combat and are hand-picked for their specialised skills in Sky Diving, Scuba Diving, Para gliding, Mountaineering, Rock craft, Snow craft, White water rafting, Martial arts, Endurance, Survival in the wild and First Aid emergency response.

We aim to set new standards of professionalism and safety in the Adventure sports arena.

About Operation Blue Freedom

Most specially abled people suffer due to their lack of mobility on land. The dream of Special Forces Adventures is to introduce the freedom of the underwater world to our specially-abled friends through Scuba Diving. To give you an idea – more than half of the Earth is covered with water. You may feel restricted by gravity on this 30% dry land mass but the 70% water world is open for you to explore, to fly through, like a bird (or a fish!). All you need is a little training.

The initiative behind Operation Blue Freedom is to introduce all our brothers from the Armed Forces Veterans fraternity, who were wounded / disabled, during the Kargil War and Counter Terrorism Ops, to the therapeutic healing power and adventure of Scuba Diving.

We harbor an unwavering passion to equip them with skills and support that would empower them to live healthy, independent and happy lives and thus inspire the world around them. A true gift of Blue Freedom!

Inaugurating Operation Blue Freedom

Operation Blue Freedom was inaugurated on 22nd August, 2019 at New Delhi by The Honorable Minister Of Sports and Youth Affairs, Mr. Kiren Rijiju and Indian Football Legend, Mr. Bhaichung Bhutia. Have a look at how we did it. After the event, here is what Mr. Kiren Rijiju has to say about Operation Blue Freedom :


"Armed forces veterans, including members of the Elite Special Forces of Para Regiment initiated a high-end adventure sports survival training titled "Op-Blue Freedom". It's a nation-wide adaptive SCUBA diving programme for people with disabilities as well as able bodied persons."

FAQs

Do participants need to bring their own swim wear ?

Yes. Participants are advised to bring their own swim wear and towels.

How would be the schedule like ?

The scuba diving tryout’s schedule is affix to 35 minutes per time slot. Participant will spend 20 minutes in water and the remaining 15 minutes in gear preparation. Each tryout slot will be limited to 4 participants at a time with one instructor and dive master present.

What should be the minimum age for participation ?

Participants above the age of 8 years old are valid to register.

What are the medical and physical fitness requirements ?

Each participant would need to fill in the medical and waiver form upon sign-up on site. Kindly note that participants with respiratory and cardiology health conditions (eg. asthma or heart issues) should inform the members of the Operation Blue Freedom prior to sign-up. Participants with myopia condition should inform the organiser as prior arrangements on a prescribed mask could be made.
